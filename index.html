03 / Strategic translation

What the catalog says

The goal is not to make a dashboard for dashboard people. It is to translate product data into brand language that founders, creative directors, merchandisers and analysts can all read.

Commerce

Apparel creates scale

Seasonal products and classics give the brand a repeatable commercial layer. This is where audience becomes revenue.

Credibility

Skateshop creates proof

The skateshop layer keeps the brand connected to the scene it speaks from. It makes the store feel like a cultural node, not only a label.

Culture

Projects create memory

The Projects archive turns drops, events, videos and collabs into continuity. That is how a brand builds mythology over time.

04 / Actionable intelligence

What a brand can do with this

The point is not only to describe Dime. It is to show how commerce data can become decisions for merchandising, content, retail and brand strategy.

Move 01 / Trust

Turn retail into proof

Skateshop is not just inventory. It validates the brand's place in the scene and gives every apparel drop more credibility.

Action: use skate products, third-party brands and hardgoods as trust signals in content, product pages and creator stories.
Move 02 / Merchandising

Split culture from conversion

Apparel, accessories and skate goods do different jobs. Treating every product as the same kind of SKU hides the strategy.

Action: build separate views for scale products, entry products, proof products and archive products.
Move 03 / Navigation

Design pages around behavior

Category pages are useful, but they do not always match how people discover fashion, skate goods or drops.

Action: test pages like full fits, low stock, skate essentials, new drop, classics and collab archive.
Move 04 / Story

Connect projects to products

Projects create meaning, but meaning becomes more valuable when it connects back to the product universe.

Action: link each project to related products, collections, partners and current commercial pathways.
Move 05 / Demand

Track scarcity after drops

"A few left" is a public demand texture. Over time, it can show which category, price band or cultural reference moves first.

Action: monitor D0, D+1, D+3 and D+7 availability by product, category, size and collection.

07 / Method

No hidden math

This is designed for attention, but the definitions stay explicit so the public does not have to guess what the numbers mean.

What is counted

  • Product card: one visible product record from the public catalog dataset.
  • Priced card: product card with a captured CAD price.
  • Non-priced card: scheduled drop or sold-out card without a captured public price.
  • Skateshop signal: product cards tagged as Skateshop or carrying Skateshop membership.

What is excluded

Non-priced cards are excluded from average, median, minimum, maximum and price-band calculations. They remain visible in status and catalog counts because they still communicate product strategy, drop mechanics or demand signals.
